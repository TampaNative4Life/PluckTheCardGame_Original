Pluck: Learn It Fast

Pluck Rules

Pluck is a three-player trick-taking game with no running score. Miss your number, and next hand somebody gets to reach in your business.

60-Second Version: Three players. Seventeen tricks. Dealer needs 7. Left needs 6. Right needs 4. Dealer picks trump. The player with the 2♣ starts the first trick. Miss your target and somebody earns the right to pluck a card exchange off you next hand.

What Makes Pluck Different

Most trick games ask you to keep score. Pluck says, “No, we’re going to make this personal.”

In Pluck, if you do not win enough tricks for your seat, another player earns a pluck against you in the next hand. That means they pass you a card face down, and you must give back your highest card in that same suit.

So no, you are not just losing a hand. You are helping somebody else load up for the next one.

Quick Learn

  • Players: 3
  • Cards in deck: 51
  • Tricks per hand: 17
  • Dealer target: 7 tricks
  • Left of dealer: 6 tricks
  • Right of dealer: 4 tricks
  • First round: no plucks
  • Lead card: 2♣ opens the hand

The Deck

Start with a standard 52-card deck and make these changes:

  • Remove the 2♦, 2♥, and 2♠
  • Keep the 2♣
  • Add both Jokers
  • One Joker is the Big Joker
  • The other is the Little Joker

That leaves 51 cards, which works cleanly for three players: 17 tricks per hand.

Aces are high.

The Goal

Every seat has a job. Hit your number and you are safe.

Seat Target
Dealer 7 tricks
Left of Dealer 6 tricks
Right of Dealer 4 tricks
Miss your number? Somebody earned the right to pluck you next round.

Setup and Deal

  1. The dealer shuffles.
  2. Another player may cut.
  3. The dealer deals all 51 cards.
  4. There are no plucks in the first round.

The Pluck

This is the soul of the game. Before a new hand starts, plucks are handled first.

Pluck Order

  • The player with the most earned plucks goes first.
  • If players are tied, start with the dealer and move left.

How a Pluck Works

  1. The plucker passes one card face down to the pluckee.
  2. This is usually a low card in that suit.
  3. The pluckee looks at it.
  4. The pluckee must return their highest card of that same suit.
Example: You pass a 5♦. They must hand back their highest diamond. That is Pluck saying, “Thank you for your cooperation.”

Joker Rules

  • Jokers do not belong to a normal suit.
  • When trump is declared, the Jokers count as trump for trick play.
  • They are always the two highest trump cards.
  • They cannot be forced out during a pluck.

In plain English: nobody smart gives away a Joker, and nobody gets bullied into returning one during a pluck.

Dealer Declares Trump

After plucks are finished, the dealer names the trump suit. This is the dealer’s built-in advantage, because the dealer has the toughest target.

Good dealers usually choose the suit they hold the most of, or the one with the most muscle. Just remember: the Big Joker and Little Joker still sit above all regular trump cards.

The Hand Always Opens the Same Way

The player holding the 2♣ leads the first trick.

  • That card is a guaranteed loser.
  • If clubs were named trump, then trump is open immediately.

Playing the 17 Tricks

  • The winner of each trick leads the next trick.
  • You must follow suit if you can.
  • If you are void in that suit, you may throw off or play trump.
  • You cannot lead trump until trump has been opened.
  • If clubs are trump, the opening 2♣ already opened trump.

Who Wins a Trick?

The trick is taken by the highest card in the led suit, unless a trump card is played. Any trump beats any non-trump. The Big Joker is highest, the Little Joker is next, and then the rest of the trump suit follows in order.

Determining Plucks for the Next Hand

After all 17 tricks are played, compare what each player won against what their seat required.

Seat Target Won Result
Dealer 7 9 Earns 2 plucks
Left of Dealer 6 5 Short by 1
Right of Dealer 4 3 Short by 1
Simple rule: Extra tricks above your target create plucks. Missing your target makes you vulnerable to getting plucked.

Deal Passes Left

After the hand is finished and plucks are determined, the deal moves one player to the left. Then everybody gets another chance to act right.

Table-Talk Summary

Need the shortest version possible? Here it is:

Make your number. Protect your hand. Do not get caught short. Because in Pluck, the next hand starts with consequences.

Pluck is easy to learn, hard to master, and real disrespectful to lazy play.
