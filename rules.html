Pluck: The Card Game

Official Rules

Pluck is a three-player trick-taking game with no running score. Miss your job this hand, and next hand somebody gets to improve their future using your cards.

Fast version: 3 players. 17 tricks. Dealer needs 7. Left needs 6. Right needs 4. Dealer declares trump. The player with the 2♣ opens the hand. If you come up short, somebody earns a pluck against you next round.
Learn Pluck in 60 Seconds →

What Makes Pluck Different

Most trick games make you track points. Pluck makes you feel consequences.

If you do not win enough tricks for your seat, another player may earn the right to pluck you before the next hand begins.

That means they pass you a card face down, and you must return your highest card in that same suit. So yes, bad play today can become somebody else’s good hand tomorrow.

Quick Learn

  • Players: 3
  • Cards: 51
  • Tricks per hand: 17
  • Dealer target: 7
  • Left target: 6
  • Right target: 4
  • First round: no plucks
  • Opening lead: 2♣

The Deck

Start with a standard 52-card deck and make these changes:

  • Remove the 2♦, 2♥, and 2♠
  • Keep the 2♣
  • Add both Jokers
  • One Joker is the Big Joker
  • One Joker is the Little Joker

That leaves 51 cards, which creates exactly 17 tricks for 3 players.

Aces are high.

The Goal

Each seat has a fixed job for the hand.

Seat Tricks Required
Dealer 7
Left of Dealer 6
Right of Dealer 4

Make your number and you are safe. Miss it, and next hand may get ugly.

Setup and Deal

  1. The dealer shuffles.
  2. Another player may cut.
  3. The dealer deals all 51 cards.
  4. There are no plucks in the first round.

The Pluck

Before a hand begins, any earned plucks are handled first.

Pluck Order

  • The player with the most earned plucks goes first.
  • If tied, start with the dealer and move left.

How a Pluck Works

  1. The plucker passes one card face down to the pluckee.
  2. This is usually a low card in that suit.
  3. The pluckee looks at the card.
  4. The pluckee must return their highest card of that same suit.
Example: You pass a 5♦. They must return their highest diamond. That is not bad luck. That is Pluck doing what Pluck does.

Joker Rules

  • Jokers are not part of a normal suit.
  • For trick play, both Jokers count as trump once trump is declared.
  • The Big Joker is highest.
  • The Little Joker is next.
  • Jokers cannot be forced out during a pluck.

In plain English: nobody smart gives one away, and nobody can make you surrender one during a pluck.

Dealer Declares Trump

After plucks are finished, the dealer names the trump suit.

This is the dealer’s advantage, because the dealer also has the toughest job. Most dealers choose the suit they hold the most of, or the one with the most power.

Opening the Hand

The player holding the 2♣ leads the first trick.

  • The 2♣ is a guaranteed loser.
  • If clubs are trump, trump is open immediately.

Playing the 17 Tricks

  • The winner of each trick leads the next one.
  • You must follow suit if you can.
  • If you are void in that suit, you may throw off or play trump.
  • You cannot lead trump until trump has been opened.
  • If clubs are trump, the opening 2♣ already opened trump.

Who Wins a Trick?

A trick is won by the highest card in the led suit unless any trump is played. Any trump beats any non-trump. The Big Joker beats everything. The Little Joker is next. After that, regular trump ranks high to low in normal order with aces high.

Determining Plucks for the Next Hand

After all 17 tricks are played, compare each player’s trick total to their target.

Seat Target Won Result
Dealer 7 9 Earns 2 plucks
Left of Dealer 6 5 Short by 1
Right of Dealer 4 3 Short by 1

The extra tricks above your target become plucks. Players who fall short are the ones at risk of being plucked.

Deal Passes Left

After the hand is finished and plucks are determined, the deal passes one player to the left.

Table-Talk Summary

Make your number. Protect your hand. Do not come up short. Because in Pluck, the next hand starts with consequences.

Pluck is easy to learn, hard to master, and disrespectful to lazy play.
