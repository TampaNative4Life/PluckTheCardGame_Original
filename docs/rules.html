Pluck Rules

Pluck is a three-player trick-taking game built on quotas, consequences, and pressure. This version does not use a traditional running score. It uses plucks earned, plucks against, and a match-ending limit.

Fast version: 3 players. 17 tricks. Dealer needs 7. Left of dealer needs 6. Right of dealer needs 4. Players pick dealer first. Dealer declares trump. The player holding 2♣ opens the first trick. If you miss your quota, the game remembers.

Learn Pluck in 60 Seconds

What Makes Pluck Different

Most trick games ask you to collect points. Pluck asks you to survive consequences.

If you do not win enough tricks for your seat, another player may earn the right to pluck you before the next hand begins. If you keep coming up short, your running total of plucks against keeps rising until you hit the match limit and you are out.

1) The Deck

Start with a standard 52-card deck. Remove the 2♦, 2♥, and 2♠. Keep the 2♣. Add both Jokers, one Big Joker and one Little Joker.

That leaves 51 cards, which creates exactly 17 tricks for 3 players.

2) Pick Dealer

Before the hand begins, players pick dealer. Each player draws one card. The lowest card becomes dealer. If there is a tie for lowest, players re-pick.

Dealer matters because the dealer seat carries the biggest quota that hand. Good luck with that.

3) Seat Targets

Every hand has three seat quotas:

Dealer: 7 tricks
Left of dealer: 6 tricks
Right of dealer: 4 tricks

The deal rotates left each hand, so the quotas rotate too. Nobody gets to hide in one easy seat forever.

4) Order of the Hand

On the first hand, there are no plucks yet. The flow is:

Pick Dealer → Deal → Trump → Play

After the first hand, the flow becomes:

Pick Dealer → Deal → Pluck → Trump → Play

5) Dealer Declares Trump

After plucks are finished, the dealer names the trump suit. The player holding 2♣ leads the first trick.

If clubs are trump, then trump is open immediately. Otherwise, normal trump-opening rules apply during play.

6) The Pluck

A pluck happens before play begins on later hands. The plucker passes one card face down to the pluckee. The pluckee must return their highest card in that same suit.

Jokers cannot be forced out during a pluck. A pluck is suit-for-suit only.

7) How Plucks Are Earned

Plucks come from the difference between what a player needed and what that player actually won.

Plucks Earned means tricks won above quota.
Plucks Against means tricks missed below quota.

Example: If your quota was 7 and you won 9 tricks, you earned 2 plucks. If your quota was 7 and you won only 4 tricks, you now have 3 plucks against.

8) The New Scoring System

This version of Pluck does not use a traditional running score. It uses a running total of plucks against.

At the start of the match, players choose a match length: 8, 10, or 12 plucks against.

That number is the danger line. The first player to reach it is out, and the match ends immediately.

9) Running Total Means Running Total

Plucks against are cumulative. They carry from hand to hand. They do not reset just because the cards were shuffled again.

Example: If you took 2 plucks against in one hand and 3 more in a later hand, your running total is now 5 plucks against.

The game keeps receipts.

10) How the Winner Is Decided

When a player hits the chosen plucks-against limit, the match ends. The winner is the player with the best differential.

Differential means: plucks earned minus plucks against.

Example: If you earned 14 plucks and only had 2 against, your differential is +12. If another player earned 2 and had 10 against, that player’s differential is -8. One player was driving the table. The other was holding on for dear life.

11) Why This Version Works

Old-school table games can run forever because nobody has a built-in reason to leave the chair. This version fixes that.

If you keep missing your quota, the pressure builds. If you keep beating your quota, your edge builds. Every hand matters. Every quota matters.

In plain English, the game now remembers who is cooking and who keeps getting cooked.
